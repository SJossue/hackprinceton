# Recall — Video Narrative (Tiger-Sentinel Pitch)


**For:** generating a ~60–90 second animated video presentation.

**Mascot:** HackPrinceton tiger — but now the tiger *is the device*. A cartoon animated with a Raspberry Pi + Logitech Brio camera; its eyes are the lens. It sits at a vantage point in a room — shelf, mantel, top of a bookcase — and keeps quiet watch.

**Tone:** warm, emotionally grounded, slightly literary. This is a product about *people*, not gadgets. The tiger is protective and silent.


---


## The elevator line


> **"Your home has a quiet memory. Just ask."**


Recall is an ambient memory companion for a physical space, built inside a cartoon tiger that sits on a shelf and watches the room. It remembers *events* — not video — and answers plain-English questions about what happened over the last hours or days. Video never leaves the tiger; only short text events cross the network. Two human-facing interfaces — a laptop dashboard and a phone-stand ambient display — are how anyone in the house asks, listens, or checks in. Privacy-first, voice-friendly second memory for elderly family members, caregivers, people with ADHD, busy households, and lab spaces.

## The problem — open the video here


Open on a warm living room. Morning light. A pill bottle on a desk, keys, a phone. A woman in her sixties (Mom) moves through the scene — she's looking for something. Pauses. Checks one pocket. Looks at the counter. Something is bothering her.


No narration yet. Just her, the quiet room, and — sitting on the top of a low bookshelf by the window — a **tiger**. Cute, watchful, still. Camera lingers on the tiger for a beat: ears up, eyes calm. A tiny amber LED pulses once, gently, beneath its collar.


**Voiceover (a calm female voice — this is the voice of Recall, delivered from the *phone-stand* across the room, not the tiger itself):**


> *"Mom misplaced her keys again. It's been happening more lately. Her daughter Sarah worries — is it normal, is it not? She can't be here all the time."*


Mom finds a sticky note. Can't read her own handwriting from earlier. Sighs.


> *"Cameras feel invasive. Reminders get ignored. The worry creeps in."*

## Meet the tiger — Recall reveals itself


Slow push toward the tiger on the shelf. Inside the tiger, implied in a quick x-ray flourish for the video, are a **Raspberry Pi** and a few wires.


Cut to a nearby phone in a small wooden stand on a side table. Its screen glows softly: a **breathing particle cloud**, sage-green, rotating slowly. Across the couch, a laptop is open showing a warm cream dashboard — a serif headline reads *"Your home has a quiet memory. Just ask."*


**Voiceover (warm, unhurried):**


> *"This is Recall. The tiger watches from where it sits — the phone and the laptop are how you talk to it. It watches, but it doesn't record. It remembers what happened — not what it saw. And when you need to know, you just ask."*


Visual: a subtle graphic animates in the lower third. Tiger → a squiggly arrow → a tiny list of "events" (*bottle placed on desk 8:02 AM, keys picked up 9:13 AM*). Bold caption: **"video never leaves the tiger."**

## The magic moment — Mom talks to Recall


Mom walks over to the phone stand. The ambient particle cloud shifts — still sage, but now tightening into **concentric rings, rippling outward**. Below the cloud, a soft italic label fades in: *"listening —"*.


**Mom:** *"Where did I put my keys?"*


The cloud morphs again — a **double helix, ochre, braiding in the air**. Italic label updates: *"remembering…"*. The tiger's head, meanwhile, stays perfectly still on its shelf. A tiny amber LED beneath its collar pulses twice, mirroring the phone's rhythm. (The tiger and the phone are the same system; the phone is just the voice.)


A beat. The particles drift far outward, leaving room in the center. A serif line of text fades in, ochre quote marks flanking it, and a calm human voice (ElevenLabs) reads it aloud:


> *"You picked up your keys from the desk at 10 PM yesterday. I haven't seen where you set them down since — they should be with you."*


Mom smiles. Looks in her coat pocket. Finds them.


**Voiceover (same calm voice, gently, back to narration):**


> *"Recall answers from what it actually saw. It never invents a story. And if it doesn't know, it says so."*

## The ambient beat — when Recall notices first


Time jump. Afternoon light. Kitchen empty. Phone screen is back to the breathing sage sphere. Tiger on the shelf, unchanged. Somewhere quietly inside it, the Pi is humming.


Suddenly the phone's particle cloud **contracts into a tight, pulsing cluster, clay-red**. A small uppercase caption fades in above the text: *"a gentle nudge."* The tiger's amber LED pulses a little more insistently. The same calm voice speaks, now with a note of concern:


> *"Mom missed her morning medication. Scheduled for 8 AM — it's been two hours since the window closed."*


Cut to Sarah (the daughter), at work, across town. Her phone buzzes with a soft text — not a scary medical alert, a warm one:


> *"Hi Sarah, just a heads-up that this morning's 8:00 medication hasn't been taken yet. When you get a chance, could you check in? Thanks, love you."*


Sarah smiles softly. Types: *"On it."*


**Voiceover:**


> *"Recall doesn't wait to be asked. When it notices something that matters, it speaks up — in a way that feels like a friend, not a hospital."*


Visual: a crisp caption appears — **"drafted by AI, reviewed by family."** The warmth is real, not manufactured.

2. **The laptop dashboard.** A cream-paper UI with a serif headline and a warm ochre Ask pill. Caption: *"where you read what happened."*

3. **The phone-stand display.** Small phone propped upright, particle cloud breathing on its screen. Caption: *"where Recall listens and speaks."*


Beneath all three, a single flowing arrow shows the only thing ever leaving the tiger: a short text event. *"bottle placed on desk 8:02 AM"* — drifts gently toward the laptop and phone.


**Voiceover (casual, a little proud):**


> *"The tiger sees everything. Inside, it understands* events *— object placed, person entered, pills taken. Only those short text events cross the room — to your laptop, to your phone. Never a second of video."*


Optional micro-beat: a split-second shot of the model selector on the laptop dashboard (Auto / K2 Think / Claude) — the two reasoning engines share answering duties. A tiny caption: *"K2 Think V2 + Claude, answering together."*

## The three "who this is for" beats


Fast montage, ~15 seconds — three short vignettes, the tiger present in frame each time (different rooms, same tiger-with-camera concept):


1. **Elderly care** — Mom finds her keys with Recall's help. Sarah sleeps easier. Tiger sits on the kitchen hutch.

2. **ADHD support** — a college student asks, *"When did I last take my ADHD meds?"* Recall answers on the phone stand: *"Around 9 AM this morning — you took them right after you put your phone on the desk."* Tiger perches on a dorm shelf between textbooks.

3. **Lab / home safety** — a grad student asks, *"Did anyone come into the lab last night?"* Recall: *"One person entered at 10:43 PM and left at 11:17 PM. They placed a book on the chair before leaving."* Tiger sits on top of a lab cabinet, unassuming.


**Voiceover (closing):**

## Notes for the animator


**Tiger character direction:**

- Cartoon-style, warm orange-and-cream, dignified posture. Think "well-loved family heirloom," not "mascot slapstick."

- The tiger **does not talk, does not move its mouth, does not emote.** It is a silent sentinel. Small details carry its presence: slow blink, ears orienting toward a sound, the soft LED pulse under its collar.

- The camera lens lives *inside* the tiger — behind a chest embroidery window, or a subtle eye-gleam, or the center of a medallion on its collar. Viewer's choice, but consistent across shots.

- Can be stylized (clean vector / storybook) — doesn't need to be photorealistic, but should read as warm and hand-drawn, not robotic.


**Pacing:**

- Target 60–90 seconds total.

- Open on emotional hook (~15 s): Mom can't find keys; tiger silently watching from the shelf.

- Reveal Recall (~10 s): the tiger *is* the device; phone and laptop are how you talk to it.

- Magic moment (~15 s): particle cloud morphs through listening → thinking → answer.

- Ambient beat with Sarah (~15 s): phone goes clay-red, warm SMS reaches the daughter.

- How-it-works architectural beat (~10 s): tiger → laptop → phone, text-only arrow.

- Who-this-is-for montage (~15 s): three rooms, three tigers, three lives quietly helped.

- Close (~5 s): golden hour on the shelf, single LED pulse, fade.


**Color palette** (matches the production app):

- **Paper / warm cream** — walls, UI backgrounds, off-whites. The world is a warm residential cream, not a cold tech white.

- **Espresso ink** — body text, dark accents, the tiger's stripes and warm shadow tones.

- **Ochre** — Recall's primary voice accent. Thinking-state particles, the Ask button, the tiger's LED, quote ornaments on answers.

- **Sage green** — medication status, the idle breathing sphere, the "all is well" moments.

- **Dusky rose-brown** — person-detection highlights, secondary warmth.

- **Clay (warm red)** — alerts. Never a neon emergency red. Think terracotta.


(If you've seen emerald/cyan/indigo in earlier mockups — those are gone. The live app is the warm palette above.)


**Typography** (match the app):

- **Fraunces** — variable serif, used for headlines, answers, state labels, the RECALL wordmark. Optical-size axis set high (`opsz: 144`) and soft axis around `SOFT: 100` for warmth.

- **Manrope** — body sans, used for UI chrome, fine print, small labels.

- Avoid mono fonts in the video. Nothing should feel like a terminal.


**Sound:**

- Ambient room sound, not music-heavy.

- ElevenLabs voice lines delivered verbatim — the quoted lines above are actual Recall output.

- Soft UI chimes on state transitions (listening → thinking, thinking → answer, idle → alert).

- Music swells only at reveal and close. Warm piano or acoustic strings, nothing electronic.


**What not to do:**

- No spinning holograms, tech-bro neon grids, or "AI brain" imagery.

- No loading bars or typing-cursor tropes — Recall's answers feel instant in the live demo; animate them the same way (typewriter reveal is fine, but keep it gentle).

- No crying-mom-finds-Alzheimer's-pamphlet moment. The tone is warm, not tragic.

- No surveillance-eye visual. The tiger is a calm presence, *not* a security cam. Its "lens" should read as a small warm detail, not a glowing red eye.

- The tiger does not speak. The voice always comes from the phone stand or laptop — those are Recall's interfaces, the tiger is the senses.


**Privacy signaling (the core pitch — make it unmissable for ~2 seconds):**

- At the "video never leaves" moment, animate the frame clearly: tiger → short text event → laptop + phone. Video stream iconography stops *inside* the tiger silhouette — nothing pixel-like crosses the arrow out.

- A clean caption: **"only short text ever leaves the tiger."**


**Real product assets to use:**

- Actual screenshots of the live laptop dashboard — warm cream paper, Fraunces headline "Your home has a quiet memory. Just ask.", ochre Ask pill, journal-style event list with room tags ("Living Room", "Kitchen"), floor plan with rooms gently glowing.

- Actual recordings of the phone `/status` ambient display — particle cloud morphing through idle / listening / thinking / answer / alert.

- Real ElevenLabs voice reading the quoted lines in the script.

- The animated cartoon tiger with the Pi implied inside, shown in illustrated form throughout the video.

- The segmented model selector (Auto / K2 Think / Claude) shown during the "two reasoning engines" beat.
